Newly Identified Diseases

Explore information about recently identified infectious diseases affecting global health.

SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19)

Identified in December 2019 in Wuhan, China, SARS-CoV-2 causes COVID-19, a respiratory illness that led to a global pandemic. It spreads through respiratory droplets and has symptoms ranging from mild fever to severe pneumonia.

Nipah Virus

First identified in 1998 in Malaysia, Nipah virus is a zoonotic pathogen transmitted from bats or pigs to humans. It causes severe encephalitis with a high mortality rate, up to 75%, and has no vaccine yet.

Mpox (Monkeypox)

Though known since 1958, Mpox gained attention with outbreaks in 2022 outside endemic areas (e.g., Africa). Caused by the monkeypox virus, it spreads via contact and causes fever, rash, and pustules.
